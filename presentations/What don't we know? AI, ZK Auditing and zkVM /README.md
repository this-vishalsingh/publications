The talk covers the security and future of Zero-knowledge proofs and applications. It highlights that ZK proofs are a valuable and innovative technology from the crypto space. 
Current ZK applications, like zkVMs and zk-rollups, hold a multi-billion dollar market value. 

Simple issues, not deep cryptographic flaws, are often the cause of major ZK application exploits. Security depends on Soundness, Completeness, and the core Zero-knowledge property of the proofs. 

It answers qns: 
- What is zkp security ?
- Where to look for bugs? What to look for ? 
- Should you pay for security audits ?
- Who can find bugs in your code?
- What are the questions to ask before deploying a zkp protocol?
- Where AI helps?
- How to be a zk-auditor/researcher/engineer?
